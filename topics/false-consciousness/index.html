Topic 6: False Consciousness

Could Ethics be a system of rules designed to keep free people down? Could it be just a bunch ideas many of us have come to believe in because we are weak, like Glaucon suggested?

Reading

  1. “False Consciousness,” Chapter 7 of Blackburn’s Being Good

Learning questions

  1. Try to develop your own example of false consciousness, if possible in an ordinary, familiar situation. That is, can you think of a situation in which someone actually does what they do for reasons that are different from the reasons they think are motivating them?
  2. What is the threat to philosophical ethics from false consciousness?
  3. What is the general outline of Nietzsche’s false consciousness view of ethics? Where have we run into a suggestion like this before, earlier in the course?
  4. How does Blackburn argue that false consciousness does not pose a serious threat to philosophical ethics?
