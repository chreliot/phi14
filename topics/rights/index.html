Topic 11: Rights

In discussing the cases in the Cases section, we found that the language of “rights” offers another way of thinking about the moral dimensions of situations. Here we consider what rights are, whether anyone has any, why we have them (if we do), and what implications they might have for other moral ideas like duty and freedom.

Reading

  1. “Freedom from the Bad,” chapter 13 of Being Good, by Simon Blackburn
  2. “Freedom and Paternalism,” chapter 14 of Being Good, by Simon Blackburn
  3. “Rights and Natural Rights,” chapter 15 of Being Good, by Simon Blackburn
