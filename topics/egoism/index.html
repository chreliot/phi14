Topic 4: Egoism

Are we completely selfish? Can we do anything genuinely altruistic—for others’ benefit? If we are selfish, like Glaucon’s descriptive argument suggests, pursuing only our own pleasure, philosophical ethics looks like a sham. Is it?

Reading

  1. “Egoism” by Simon Blackburn in Being Good (eBook through Axinn Library login, or search for “Being Good” directly in the Library Catalog “Discovery” search box), pages 29–37.
  2. brief excerpts from “Psychological Egoism” by Joel Feinberg (1958), available on Blackboard > Course Documents.

Learning questions

  1. What’s a reason to think that human beings are selfish?
  2. What’s a reason to think that human beings are completely selfish?
  3. If we are completely selfish, why would that pose a problem for philosophical ethics?

Consider

  1. How is the idea that human beings are selfish related to other areas besides philosophy? Does it connect with economics, biology, or psychology, for example? Does theory in those areas support it or undermine it?
