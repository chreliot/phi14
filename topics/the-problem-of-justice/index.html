Topic 1: The Problem of Justice

Are there any reasons not to behave unjustly, if we can get away with it? We examine how Plato asks this question through a “thought experiment.” Plato has his character Glaucon stake out a challenging position on this question.

Reading

  1. Plato, “The Ring of Gyges,” an excerpt from his longer work Republic.

Reading questions

  1. The text takes the form of a conversation, though with extended speeches by each speaker. As the text opens (and for most of it, except briefly where Socrates interrupts, as highlighted by the sidenote) the speaker is Glaucon. What topic does Glaucon say he’s going to talk about?
  2. What, then, is Glaucon’s opening position on this topic in the first two paragraphs?
  3. What does Glaucon ask his interlocutors (= the people he is speaking with) to imagine?
  4. How does the story Glaucon tells support his point?
  5. What does Glaucon conclude about human psychology or human nature with respect to doing what’s right?
  6. What does Glaucon conclude is best for human beings, at least according to what people (or, “they”) say?

See also

  • “Plato’s Ethics and Politics in The Republic” in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, by Eric Brown, presents the larger context of the book from which this excerpt is taken
