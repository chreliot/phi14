Topic 8: Kantianism

German philosopher Immanuel Kant, working in the years before 1800, produced an audacious attempt to ground morality in the idea of the “good will.” We will look at how he does this in the book where he introduces his understanding of morality. Then we consider how we might apply Kantian thinking to particular cases. Finally, we identify criticisms of Kantian reasoning about morality and ways of addressing them.

Reading

  2. “The Categorial Imperative,” chapter 18 of Being Good
