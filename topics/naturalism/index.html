Topic 5: Naturalism

Does evolution favor selfish behavior as organisms pursue survival and reproduction in a competitive environment? Alternatively, what if we are ethical, but only because ethics is merely a behavior we’ve evolved to exhibit? Would that make it a sham? Can competing organisms rationally cooperate?

Reading

  1. “Evolutionary Theory” by Simon Blackburn, in Being Good, pages 37–43.
  2. Listen to “Cooperation and Conflict,” by Philosophy Talk Starters via SoundCloud. (10 min, 35 seconds)1

Learning questions

  1. What are two ways natural selection might threaten ethics?
  2. What are Blackburn’s three cautions about thinking about evolution and ethics?
  3. How might natural selection promote ethics and cooperative behavior?
