Notes on Relativism

In what way are moral claims true/false? [for example, “Murder is wrong."]

“Chocolate is delicious.” Is that true? Why or why not? “The Earth is round.” Is that true? Why or why not?

Ways of being true/false (or not)

  1. Realism - Statements are true when they match reality (false when they don’t).
  2. Non-realism - Statements neither match nor fail to match reality; any opinion is as good as any other.
  3. Relativism - Statements can be true/false relative to their believers — true “for them.” There is no objective/standard.

“Murder is wrong”

  • realist: “True! That’s a fact in the universe.”
  • non-realist: “That’s my feeling about it.”
  • relativist: “That’s true for us.”

What’s possibly compelling/appealing about understanding ethics as relative?

  • Morality is nuanced. It varies by situation.
  • Makes sense of cultural differences, in space and time.
  • Makes rightness/wrongness a function of cultural tradition.
