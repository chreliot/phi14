Notes on God

Two threats from God

First threat

Fyodr Dostoevksy’s Brothers Karamazov, roughly: “If God is dead, everything is permitted” (= if there is no god, nothing is really unethical/wrong)

If God is a legislator of morality, then if there’s no God, there are or could be no rules of morality. Nothing is wrong.

Possible answers

  • God exists, and God legislates morality, and God backs up moral rules with reward/punishment.
  • Though God doesn’t exist, atheists nonetheless behave well because they find moral truths elsewhere.
    • BUT: maybe they just behave well because they’re forced to by society? This was part of Glaucon’s worry.

Second threat

If God exists and legislates morality, then is there anything more to discuss in philosophical ethics? If we have all the answers already, what are we doing discussing ethics?

Possible answers

  1. Texts presenting God’s will may require interpretation.
  2. Religions have different ideas about what God legislates.
    • BUT: Religious diversity might be superficial, built around a common/shared core.
    • BUT: Some religions might be wrong.
  3. Religious texts are incomplete about moral instruction (how the rules apply to the details of situations), and so require discussion.
  4. Even those having moral truths from God may need to discuss those truths with other non-believers to make society work.
