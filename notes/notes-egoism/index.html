Notes on Egoism

Psychological egoism

= “the doctrine that the only thing anyone is capable of desiring or pursuing ultimately (as an end in itself) is his own self interest.” (Feinberg)

Why a threat to philosophical ethics?

Exclusively pursuing our self interest precludes pursuing others' interests. And pursuing others' interests (“altruism”) is one thing Ethics should prescribe.

Reason egoism might be true?

We can (cynically) analyze every possible case of potentially/nominally-altruistic behavior as really merely selfish.

Reasons egoism might not be true

  • There’s not good evidence for general psychological egoism as a scientific theory.

  • Many times the pleasure we get out of doing something derives from our desiring something else other than pleasure itself. Otherwise, we wouldn’t have wanted to do that thing. Otherwise, we wouldn’t derive pleasure from it! The other thing we desire can be altruistic.

So, we don’t have good reason to think we never behave altruistically or always behave selfishly.

Open questions:

How much of our behavior is selfish? How much is altruistic? Could we know?
